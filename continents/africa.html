Barbary Macaque Macaca sylvanus Algeria 20-25 years Endangered The only macaque species found outside Asia, native to North Africa, and known for its social behavior.
Fennec Fox Vulpes zerda Algeria 10-14 years Least Concern Famous for its large ears, which help dissipate heat, this nocturnal fox is well-adapted to desert life.
Dama Gazelle Nanger dama Algeria 12-15 years Critically Endangered One of the largest gazelles, known for its slender build and reddish-brown coat, found in desert and savanna habitats.
Angolan Giraffe Giraffa camelopardalis angolensis Angola 25-30 years Vulnerable Known for its distinct pattern and long neck, this giraffe species is mostly found in savannas.
Giant Sable Antelope Hippotragus niger variani Angola 17-19 years Critically Endangered An iconic animal of Angola, known for its striking, curved horns and black coat in males.
African Elephant Loxodonta africana Angola 60-70 years Endangered The largest land animal, essential for ecosystem engineering, roaming the savannas and forests of Angola.
West African Lion Panthera leo senegalensis Benin 8-14 years Vulnerable This lion subspecies is native to West Africa and plays a critical role in the balance of the ecosystem.
African Forest Elephant Loxodonta cyclotis Benin 60-70 years Critically Endangered Smaller than the savanna elephant, this forest-dwelling elephant is crucial for seed dispersal in West African forests.
Patas Monkey Erythrocebus patas Benin 15-20 years Least Concern Known for its reddish-brown coat and remarkable running speed, making it one of the fastest primates.
Kalahari Lion Panthera leo Botswana 10-14 years Vulnerable Known for its larger size and mane, adapted to the harsh conditions of the Kalahari Desert.
African Wild Dog Lycaon pictus Botswana 10-12 years Endangered A social and efficient hunter, known for its colorful coat and unique pack structure.
Lechwe Antelope Kobus leche Botswana 10-15 years Least Concern Known for its unique ability to move through water, it is commonly found in wetlands and floodplains.
Cross River Gorilla Gorilla gorilla diehli Cameroon 35-50 years Critically Endangered One of the rarest gorilla subspecies, found in the dense forests of the Cameroon-Nigeria border.
Goliath Frog Conraua goliath Cameroon 15 years Endangered The largest frog species, known for its impressive size and found only in Cameroon and Equatorial Guinea.
Mandrill Mandrillus sphinx Cameroon 20 years Vulnerable Known for its vibrant coloration and expressive face, it is one of Africa’s most striking primates.
Saharan Silver Ant Cataglyphis bombycina Chad Few months (worker ants) Not Evaluated Known for its metallic silver body, it withstands extreme desert temperatures and is one of the fastest ants.
Addax Antelope Addax nasomaculatus Chad 19-25 years Critically Endangered Also called the white antelope, it is adapted to desert life and can survive long periods without water.
Dromedary Camel Camelus dromedarius Chad 40-50 years Domesticated Essential for transportation in desert areas, known for its single hump and endurance in harsh climates.
Okapi Okapia johnstoni Democratic Republic of Congo 20–30 years Endangered Often called the "forest giraffe," this unique animal is native to Congo’s dense forests.
Bonobo Pan paniscus Democratic Republic of Congo 40 years Endangered Closely related to humans, these highly social primates are known for their peaceful societies.
African Grey Parrot Psittacus erithacus Democratic Republic of Congo 40-60 years Endangered Renowned for its intelligence and mimicry skills, it's one of the most well-known African parrots.
Nile Crocodile Crocodylus niloticus Egypt 70-100 years Least Concern One of the largest crocodile species, found throughout the Nile River and known for its powerful bite.
Egyptian Vulture Neophron percnopterus Egypt 21 years Endangered This scavenger is known for its white plumage and intelligent use of rocks to break eggs.
Rüppell's Fox Vulpes rueppellii Egypt 6-10 years Least Concern A small, desert-adapted fox with large ears to dissipate heat, found throughout Egypt’s arid regions.
Gelada Theropithecus gelada Ethiopia 20 years Least Concern Also known as the "bleeding-heart monkey," this primate is native to the Ethiopian Highlands.
Grevy's Zebra Equus grevyi Kenya 20-25 years Endangered The largest zebra species, known for its narrow stripes, found mainly in Kenya’s semi-arid areas.
Reticulated Giraffe Giraffa camelopardalis reticulata Kenya 20-25 years Endangered Known for its distinctive, web-like coat pattern, the reticulated giraffe roams northern Kenya.
Aye-Aye Daubentonia madagascariensis Madagascar 20-23 years Endangered A nocturnal lemur with a unique long middle finger for foraging, native to Madagascar’s forests.
Fossa Cryptoprocta ferox Madagascar 15-20 years Vulnerable Madagascar’s largest carnivorous mammal, resembling a cross between a cat and a mongoose.
Indri Indri indri Madagascar 15-18 years Critically Endangered The largest lemur species, known for its loud calls and strong bonds in family groups.
Nigerian-Cameroon Chimpanzee Pan troglodytes ellioti Nigeria 33-40 years Endangered This unique chimpanzee subspecies is known for its complex social behavior and communication.
West African Manatee Trichechus senegalensis Nigeria 30-40 years Vulnerable Also known as the sea cow, it is found in rivers and estuaries in West Africa and is vital for aquatic ecosystems.
African Rock Python Python sebae Nigeria 20-30 years Least Concern One of Africa’s largest snakes, found near water bodies, and known for its strength and size.
